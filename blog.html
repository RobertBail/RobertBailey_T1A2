"Thoughts on this Coder Academy course, comparison with edX/University of Adelaide" Posted: 21-03-2024 4:30PM

I decided to do this Coder Academy course at the moment, because some of the edX/University of Adelaide course was a bit rushed. It had a very good main instructor, and the classes (my class at least) were smaller, 15 people, reduced to about 10 when people dropped out. So it was a bit easier to ask questions directly to him, but some of the topics really needed 2 or 3 weeks to understand probably, instead of the one week, new topic each week. Mid last year I was deciding whether to do Coder Academy or edX/University of Adelaide or both, and by December last year, I decided I should probably do both, to familiarize with some of the topics some more, and a bit better/Australian networking and the placements. Also I have sometimes looked up some of the jobs, eg. "entry level" jobs, related to IT/coding, which often still require some experience, eg. 6 months or 2 years, so another reason I decided to do this extra 10 months of study/experience. And it looks like a few things will be covered in this course which weren't covered in edX/University of Adelaide, such as Ruby and Python.

Photo by Clément Hélardot on Unsplash

When I was in school and uni 15-20 years ago, I didn't think I would be working in IT so much, and wasn't so much interested in it. But in the last 7 or 8 years I have been involved in a few IT or coding-related projects, projects which touched on this, such as SEO or other web content. And I'm aware this would be an industry with increasing demand for workforce, such as for working with AI. So this is the first time I have properly learnt coding, the full process, and I could see myself getting more-so into IT work, as a suitable occupation for me. I have had some other interests for jobs over the years but not been accepted so far, such as aviation, media, film. I've never had the money (up to $100,000 approx. for commercial licence) for training as a pilot, and wasn't as good with maths and physics, B or C level, same for the ATC test. I have expressed interest in ramp agent a few times but not been accepted, probably because I'm of smaller build, so not as good for baggage/freight handling, and I wasn't as sure about the 24/7 rotating roster, since I have a dodgy sleeping pattern. I'll also probably never be good with customer service roles, as I possibly have "mild" Aspergers according to my mother and some tests. But IT/coding seems quite suitable for me. I don't mind spending hours completing/creating something and problem-solving.

"Memories of computing and Internet usage" Posted: 22-03-2024 10:00AM

Photo by Fredy Jacob on Unsplash

Even though apparently I still look younger, or get mistaken for younger, I'm 36 in July. I have a few more lines on my face now, and veins on my legs. Growing up in the 90s and early 2000s was an interesting time for technology. There seemed to be a new or improved thing every year, with phones, computers, Internet and music players. Eg. I remember using floppy disks up until 2004, before switching to USBs. I think I used a Discman for 1 or 2 years in 2003-2005 approx. before using an MP3 player. My parents were also a bit daggy, a few years behind the times, and I had to pay for it myself often, eg. I didn't get a mobile phone till 2004, after I got my first job at Bi-Lo. I first used computers from 1993 onwards, in "computing" class. I think there were only 6 computers in the eventual "computer room" at that primary school, so we had to pair up, and only half the class at a time. In 1995 onwards, they got 20 or 30 computers for a full class. I started using the Internet from about 1998/10 years old onwards, I think mainly looking up aircraft images and listening to Eiffel 65 songs. This was mainly at school, after-school care or at internet cafes, eg. the internet cafe at my dad's work. It wasn't until late 99/early 2000 when my family got a household PC. I remember first discovering XXX material on the Internet at about 12, probably from my facscination with Anna Kournikova at the time, looking up images of her, which led onto some XXX material, but with dial-up internet it took about half an hour to download a 10 minute clip, and I could only do it occasionally while my parents were out, then making sure to delete it. It wasn't until 2004/2005, in my experience, when the Internet became more of a 24/7, efficient, streamlined system, things like Google, YouTube, Facebook and MySpace leading the way.
